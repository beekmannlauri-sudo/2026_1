A brief of elections, presidencies, strategies, and excise duty changes that shape alcohol and drug policy across the Nordic and Baltic region.

Key 2026 drivers

Nordic Council presidency

Finland (and Åland) holds the Nordic Council presidency in 2026.

Source

Nordic Council of Ministers

Denmark and the Faroe Islands hold the Nordic Council of Ministers presidency in 2026, with priorities including children and youth, resilience, and cross-border challenges.

Source

Nordic Dimension Partnership (NDPHS)

On 1 January 2026, Norway took over the NDPHS Chairship with Finland serving as Co-Chair, shaping regional cooperation on public health and social well-being.

Source

Arctic Council

The Kingdom of Denmark holds the Arctic Council Chairship for 2025–2027, with a focus on Indigenous Peoples, sustainable development, and maintaining the Arctic as a region of stability and constructive cooperation.

Source
NB8

NB8 and Baltic cooperation

Estonia chairs NB8 and the Baltic cooperation formats in 2026, shaping regional coordination on health and security-related policies.

Source

Council of the EU presidencies

Cyprus (January–June) and Ireland (July–December) hold the rotating Council of the EU presidency in 2026, influencing legislative priorities in health, justice, and internal affairs.

Source

EU drugs policy reset

Implementation of the EU Drugs Strategy 2026–2030 and its Action Plan begins, setting the framework for prevention, treatment, and supply reduction across Member States.

Source
